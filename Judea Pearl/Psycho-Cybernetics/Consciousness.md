### ULTIMATE GOAL: Consciousness is a blueprint of its own 'Software'.


From the perspective of theoretical physics, Dr. Maxwell Maltz’s work in *Psycho-Cybernetics* creates an elegant operational model of the human mind. He posits that the human brain and nervous system function as a sophisticated goal-striving servo-mechanism, not unlike the guidance systems found in ballistics or modern computing.  
Here is a concise analysis of the fifteen chapters, dissecting the mechanics of this system as laid out in the text:

### 1\. The Self-Image: Your Key to a Better Life

Maltz identifies the **self-image** as the baseline calibration for the entire human system. It is a premise, a foundation upon which your personality and behavior are built 1\. From a mechanical standpoint, this self-image defines the "area of the possible," acting as a governor that limits individual accomplishment 2\. You cannot outperform the parameters of your self-image; however, this image can be altered, thereby expanding the system's potential output 3\.

### 2\. Discovering the Success Mechanism Within You

We must recognize that the human brain and nervous system constitute a **teleological** (goal-oriented) mechanism 4\. This system operates on cybernetic principles: it steers toward a target using feedback data to correct its course 5\. The forebrain selects the target coordinates, and the automatic mechanism (the servo-mechanism) executes the trajectory 6\. It functions as a guidance system to reach a known goal or as a computer to discover an unknown answer 7, 8\.

### 3\. Imagination: The First Key to Your Success Mechanism

Imagination is the programming language of this servo-mechanism. Experimental data confirms that the human nervous system cannot distinguish between an actual physical experience and one that is vividly imagined 9\. By visualizing a specific outcome, we provide the mechanism with a target data set. Mental practice—simulation—can improve performance metrics in physical reality, as demonstrated in experiments involving dart throwing and basketball 10\.

### 4\. Dehypnotize Yourself from False Beliefs

False beliefs act as erroneous data inputs or "hypnotic" commands that restrict the system's functionality 11\. Just as a hypnotized subject’s physical strength is limited by the suggestion of weakness, individuals are limited by negative self-definitions 12\. To optimize performance, one must analyze these beliefs rationally and "dehypnotize" the system, replacing false data with the truth of one's inherent capability 13\.

### 5\. How to Utilize the Power of Rational Thinking

Rational thought serves as the operator of the machine. It is the control knob 14\. The conscious mind must analyze input data, accept what is true, reject what is false, and select the target goal 15, 16\. However, once the vector is calculated and the decision made, the operator must relinquish control to the automatic mechanism; attempting to force the calculation via conscious effort (willpower) introduces friction and error 17\.

### 6\. Relax and Let Your Success Mechanism Work for You

Stress and excessive conscious effort "jam" the creative machinery 18\. For the servo-mechanism to function efficiently, the system must be relaxed. Maltz advises doing the "worrying" (calculation of variables) before placing a bet, but once the wheel turns, one must dismiss responsibility for the outcome and allow the mechanism to run free 19\. Creative insights and effortless performance occur when the conscious mind creates a vacuum of tension, often during sleep or relaxation 20\.

### 7\. You Can Acquire the Habit of Happiness

Happiness is defined medically as a state of mind where thinking is pleasant a majority of the time 21\. It is not a moral reward but a functional necessity; the system operates at peak efficiency (healthier, more creative) when happy 22\. Happiness is a mental habit—an internal attitude independent of external variables 23\. One must stop reacting to external stimuli as a slave and instead generate an internal state of satisfaction 24\.

### 8\. Ingredients of the “Success-Type” Personality and How to Acquire Them

A "Success-Type" personality is a system functioning with optimal parameters. Maltz uses the acronym **S-U-C-C-E-S-S** to define these coordinates: **S**ense of direction (goals), **U**nderstanding (distinguishing fact from opinion), **C**ourage (risk-taking), **C**ompassion (respect for others), **E**steem (self-value), **S**elf-Confidence (memory of past success), and **S**elf-Acceptance (accepting the actual self over a fictitious ideal) 25\.

### 9\. The Failure Mechanism: How to Make It Work for You Instead of Against You

Conversely, the "Failure-Type" personality exhibits symptoms that act as warning signals or negative feedback. The acronym **F-A-I-L-U-R-E** identifies these: **F**rustration, **A**ggressiveness (misdirected), **I**nsecurity, **L**oneliness, **U**ncertainty, **R**esentment, and **E**mptiness 26\. These are merely red lights on the dashboard; they indicate the system is off-course and requires corrective action, rather than being fixed states of being 27\.

### 10\. How to Remove Emotional Scars, or How to Give Yourself an Emotional Face-Lift

Emotional scars act as calcification or a hardened shell, intended for protection but ultimately isolating the individual and creating a distorted self-image 28, 29\. To restore system connectivity, one must apply the scalpel of therapeutic forgiveness 30\. This involves cancelling debts and giving up the sense of condemnation, thereby removing the tension that creates the scar tissue 31\.

### 11\. How to Unlock Your Real Personality

Personality is the energy radiated by the real self. "Inhibition" is defined as excessive negative feedback—the control mechanism is overly sensitive to error, causing the system to oscillate or freeze 32, 33\. To unlock personality, one must practice **disinhibition**—reducing the sensitivity of the feedback loop to allow for spontaneous flow and action without excessive pre-calculation 34, 35\.

### 12\. Do-It-Yourself Tranquilizers That Bring Peace of Mind

Disturbance is caused by an over-response to external stimuli (the "bell") 36\. One can construct a "psychic umbrella" or buffer zone by conditioning the system to delay or ignore the response signal 37, 38\. Maltz suggests building a "quiet room" in the mind—a decompression chamber to reset the system’s equilibrium between tasks, preventing emotional carry-over 39, 40\.

### 13\. How to Turn a Crisis into a Creative Opportunity

A crisis is a situation where the stakes create over-motivation, which can inhibit performance. To immunize the system, one should practice in a "shadowboxing" simulation where pressure is absent, thereby laying down clear neural tracks 41, 42\. In the actual event, excitement should be reinterpreted not as fear, but as a power infusion to be utilized aggressively toward the goal 43, 44\.

### 14\. How to Get That Winning Feeling

The system stores successful action patterns as "engrams" (neural patterns) in the brain tissue 45\. By vividly recalling past successes, one reactivates these specific engrams and the accompanying "winning feeling" 46\. This feeling acts as a thermometer, indicating the mechanism is set for success 47\. One can synthesize this state by ignoring past failures (negative data) and replaying success records 48\.

### 15\. More Years of Life and More Life in Your Years

The human entity is animated by a "Life Force" or adaptation energy, which is distinct from caloric energy 49, 50\. This force is teleological; it requires a goal to function. To extend life and vitality, one must create a "need" for more life by looking forward to future goals 51\. Retirement from goal-striving often leads to rapid system decay; conversely, maintaining creativity and enthusiasm keeps the system biologically younger 52, 53\.
